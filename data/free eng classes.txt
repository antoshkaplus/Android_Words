Subject(don't use do) and object(use do) questions

  Who does Romeo love? Romeo loves Juliet.
  Who loves Romeo? Juliet loves Romeo.
  

Phrasal Verb:
if you "look after" something, you take care of it. 
  (a child, the planet, a pet)
if you "look at" something, you move your eyes so you can see it. ()
  (a painting, a view, a computure )
if you "look for" something, you try to find it.
  (your keys, a lost dog, a job)
if you "look around" somewhere, you see what is there.
  (a museum, a new house, a foregn city)
if you "look up" something, you try and find it in a book, a list, 
  or using a computer.
  (a word, bus times, a telephone number)
if you "look into" something, you try to figure out why it
  happened.
  (a problem, the cause of an accident)
if you "look up to" someone, you admire(любоваться,восторгаться,
  рассматривать с восхищением) them.
  (a teacher, your mother, your role model)

Action and Stative verbs:
  action verbs:
  - describe things that happen
  - used in the present simple OR present continuous
  stative verbs:
  - describe states like being, possessing, feeling and thinking
  - are NOT usually used with the present continuous
  
  common stative verbs: agree, appear, be, believe, belong, 
    contain, like, feel, fit, forget, have, hate, last, love,
    matter, mean, need, own, prefer, realize, remember, understand,
    want
